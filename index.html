Voice AI →

Why voice-first interfaces will eat SaaS workflows

Voice AI isn't just a wrapper on STT + LLM + TTS. The real moat lies in orchestration — latency budgets, turn-taking, barge-in handling, and context persistence across multi-turn conversations. Most "voice AI" startups are thin wrappers; the winners will own the runtime.

Orchestration Latency Platform vs. Wrapper
RL Evals →

Evaluation is the bottleneck for RL-tuned agents

RLHF got us ChatGPT. But the next leap — autonomous agents that reliably complete multi-step tasks — is gated on eval infrastructure. You can't reward what you can't measure. The companies building robust, domain-specific eval harnesses will define the agent quality ceiling.

RLHF Agent Evals Reward Shaping
Developer Infra →

AI-native dev tools need new primitives, not plugins

Bolting AI onto existing IDEs is a local maximum. The real unlock is rethinking the development loop itself — intent-driven specs, continuous verification, and human-in-the-loop at the architecture level, not the line-edit level. The IDE of 2027 looks nothing like today's.

AI-native IDEs Spec-driven Dev Human-in-the-loop
India SaaS →

India's vertical SaaS moment is now — but only for builders who go deep

Horizontal SaaS in India is a race to the bottom on pricing. The opportunity is in vertical plays — healthcare, logistics, lending — where domain expertise compounds into defensibility. The playbook: own the workflow, embed payments, become the system of record.
